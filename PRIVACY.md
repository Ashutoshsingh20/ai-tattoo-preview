# Privacy Policy

**Last Updated**: March 23, 2026

## Overview

AI Tattoo Preview ("we", "our", "the app") is committed to protecting your privacy. This policy explains how we handle your data.

## TL;DR

- ✅ **We do NOT store your photos** - everything happens on your device
- ✅ **We do NOT track you** (in the MVP)
- ✅ **No account required** - completely anonymous usage
- ✅ **Open source** - [inspect the code](https://github.com/Ashutoshsingh20/ai-tattoo-preview)

## What We Do NOT Collect

- ❌ Photos or images you capture
- ❌ Camera data
- ❌ Biometric information
- ❌ Personal identifying information
- ❌ Location data
- ❌ Device identifiers

## What We DO Collect (Future)

When we add analytics (opt-in only):
- Page views (anonymized)
- Feature usage stats (no personal data)
- Error logs (crash reports)
- Browser type (for compatibility)

## Camera Permissions

The app requests camera access to:
1. Capture your body placement area
2. Preview tattoo overlays

**Important**:
- All processing happens **locally** in your browser
- Photos **never leave your device**
- We cannot see or access your images
- Deny permission = app won't work (camera is core feature)

## Third-Party Services

**Current (MVP)**: None

**Future**:
- **Hosting**: Vercel/Netlify (static files only, no data stored)
- **Analytics**: Plausible or Fathom (privacy-first, GDPR compliant)
- **Payments**: Stripe (if we add paid features)
- **Error Tracking**: Sentry (crash reports only)

## Data Retention

Since we don't store anything, there's nothing to retain or delete.

**Saved Previews**: Downloaded to your device. We never see them.

## Your Rights (GDPR/CCPA)

You have the right to:
- Access your data (there is none)
- Delete your data (there is none)
- Opt out of tracking (we don't track yet)
- Export your data (nothing to export)

## Children's Privacy

This app is not intended for users under 18 (tattoo age restrictions apply). We do not knowingly collect data from minors.

## Cookies

**Current**: None (MVP uses no cookies)

**Future**: If we add authentication, we'll use session cookies. You can disable them in browser settings.

## Security

Since all processing is client-side:
- No server-side data breaches possible
- Your photos never traverse the internet
- Use HTTPS to prevent man-in-the-middle attacks
